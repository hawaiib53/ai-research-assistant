TRAVEL RESEARCH REPORT

Top Spring & Fall Vacation Destinations

Prepared for a frequent traveler based in Sun Prairie, WI, with limited vacation windows and a preference for spring or fall travel · Departing Minneapolis–St. Paul International Airport (MSP) · July 2026

Objective

Identify the five best-fit destinations for a spring or fall vacation, prioritizing favorable shoulder-season weather, along with flight time and round-trip cost from Minneapolis–St. Paul International Airport (MSP). The list balances quick, low-cost domestic options against longer-haul international destinations, since a limited travel window makes flight time a real constraint. MSP's large Delta hub gives access to nonstop routes that a smaller regional airport would not have, which opens up international options that would otherwise require a connection.

Destination Details

1. Charleston, South Carolina

Best time to goSpring (April–May) or fall (September–November)
Flight time from MSPNonstop, 2h 41m – 2h 50m on Delta or Sun Country
Avg. round-trip airfare$200 – $320 round trip, occasionally as low as $107–$197 on sale fares
Typical weatherLow-to-mid 70s°F (21–24°C) daytime, mild humidity, coastal breeze

Charleston is the easiest destination on this list to fit into a short window — MSP has daily nonstop service with no connection required in either direction. Spring and fall both bring comfortable, humidity-light weather and avoid the brutal heat of a Lowcountry summer. It pairs history (the historic district, plantations), beaches (Folly Beach, Sullivan's Island), and food without requiring more than a long weekend.

Highlights:

  • Historic district walking tours and antebellum architecture
  • Folly Beach and Sullivan's Island for a beach day
  • Award-winning Lowcountry and Southern food scene

2. Asheville, North Carolina

Best time to goFall — early to mid-October for peak foliage
Flight time from MSPNonstop, 2h 15m – 2h 23m on Sun Country, Delta, or Allegiant
Avg. round-trip airfare$110 – $280 round trip; sale fares have dipped as low as $107
Typical weatherDaily swing from around 38°F (3°C) at higher elevations to mid-60s°F (18°C) in town; crisp air, peak color mid-October

Asheville is the shortest and often the cheapest nonstop flight on the list, making it a strong option if the travel window is very tight — it's an easy there-and-back within a single weekend. The Blue Ridge Parkway and surrounding Blue Ridge Mountains put on one of the best fall foliage displays in the country, and the town itself has a strong food, brewery, and arts scene to fill out a short trip.

Highlights:

  • Blue Ridge Parkway scenic drives for peak foliage
  • Biltmore Estate and gardens
  • Dense craft-brewery and farm-to-table food scene downtown

3. Amsterdam, Netherlands

Best time to goSpring — mid-April through early May for tulip season
Flight time from MSPNonstop, 7h 55m – 8h 20m on Delta or KLM
Avg. round-trip airfare$650 – $1,050 round trip; cheapest fares tend to cluster in April, with occasional deals near $850
Typical weatherDaytime highs around 46–64°F (8–18°C), warming through the season; changeable with occasional showers and wind

Amsterdam has nonstop MSP service, which is a meaningful advantage over most other European gateways from the Upper Midwest — no connection means the entire flight budget goes toward time in-destination rather than a layover. Tulip season peaks in the second half of April, when Keukenhof Gardens and the flower fields south of the city are at their most colorful, and King's Day on April 27 adds a lively citywide event to the trip. May tends to bring milder, more predictable weather if a slightly later trip works better.

Highlights:

  • Keukenhof Gardens and the Bollenstreek tulip fields
  • Canal cruises through the Grachtengordel and Jordaan district
  • Van Gogh Museum and Rijksmuseum, with shorter lines than summer

4. Reykjavík, Iceland

Best time to goFall — September into early October
Flight time from MSPNonstop, 5h 55m – 6h 40m on Icelandair or Delta
Avg. round-trip airfare$400 – $700 round trip; October is often the cheapest month to fly, with fares as low as $350–$400
Typical weatherDaytime highs around 41–49°F (5–9°C), crisp and cooling through the season, with frequent rain and wind

Reykjavík is the shortest transatlantic flight on this list — under six and a half hours nonstop — which makes it one of the most time-efficient ways to fit a genuinely different landscape into a short window. September brings milder temperatures and longer daylight for hiking and sightseeing, while late September into October brings back dark enough skies for the season's first northern lights without the deep cold of winter. Iceland's shoulder season also sees smaller crowds and lower prices than summer at the major sights.

Highlights:

  • Golden Circle day trip: &Thorn;ingvellir, Geysir, and Gullfoss
  • Blue Lagoon or Sky Lagoon geothermal spas
  • Early-season northern lights viewing away from city light pollution

5. Kyoto, Japan

Best time to goSpring (late March–early April, cherry blossoms) or fall (mid–late November, foliage)
Flight time from MSPNonstop MSP–Tokyo Haneda (HND), roughly 12h 40m – 13h, plus about a 2.5-hour onward train or connecting flight to Kyoto
Avg. round-trip airfare$900 – $1,450 round trip to Tokyo; connects onward to Kyoto via Shinkansen bullet train (about 2h 15m from Tokyo Station)
Typical weather50–68°F (10–20°C), mild and generally dry in both target windows

Kyoto is the longest and most expensive trip on this list, so it makes the most sense if the travel window can stretch to a week or more to justify the flight time — though the nonstop MSP–Haneda route is a genuine advantage, since most other Midwest airports require at least one connection to reach Japan. In exchange, it offers two distinct shoulder-season experiences: sakura (cherry blossom) season in spring, at its peak in the first ten days of April, or the maple-leaf foliage of momiji season, which peaks in late November. Both draw crowds, so booking accommodations several months ahead is worthwhile.

Highlights:

  • Arashiyama bamboo grove and Fushimi Inari's torii gates
  • Higashiyama district's preserved streets and temples
  • Philosopher's Path for cherry blossoms or fall foliage

How to Use This Report

Given a limited travel window built around a Monday–Friday, 8 a.m.–5 p.m. Central work schedule, Asheville and Charleston are the lowest-friction picks — short nonstop flights, low cost, and no jet lag, so a long weekend is enough to make the trip worthwhile. Reykjavík is the most efficient international option: a single overnight nonstop flight and a moderate budget for a destination that feels entirely different from the Midwest. Amsterdam is the most balanced European option, with nonstop MSP access and a globally recognizable spring event in tulip season. Kyoto rewards a longer trip length given the flight time invested, and MSP's nonstop Haneda route makes it more reachable from the Twin Cities than from most other U.S. airports outside the coasts.
